## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes movie data to identify key factors that influence revenue, ratings, and overall performance. The goal is to extract actionable insights that can support decision-making in the entertainment industry.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
The movie industry produces a large number of films, but not all achieve financial success. This project aims to analyze movie data and determine which factors contribute most to a movie’s success.

---

## ❓ Key Questions
- What factors influence movie revenue?
- Is there a relationship between budget and revenue?
- Do higher-rated movies generate more revenue?
- Which genres perform best financially?
- How does popularity impact revenue?

---

## 📊 Analysis Performed

### 🔹 Univariate Analysis
- Distribution of revenue  
- Distribution of ratings  
- Genre frequency  

### 🔹 Bivariate Analysis
- Revenue vs Budget  
- Revenue vs Rating  
- Genre vs Revenue  

### 🔹 Multivariate Analysis
- Combined effect of budget, genre, and ratings on revenue  
- Correlation analysis  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
- High-budget movies tend to generate higher revenue  
- Certain genres consistently outperform others  
- Higher-rated movies generally perform better financially  
- Popularity strongly influences revenue  

---

## 📈 Conclusion
The analysis shows that budget, genre, ratings, and popularity are key drivers of movie success. These insights can help in better planning and investment decisions.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Invest in high-performing genres  
- Focus on improving movie quality to boost ratings  
- Allocate budgets strategically for higher returns  
- Enhance marketing efforts to increase popularity
